Predicting World
Happiness

Summary:

In this project, we wanted to answer the question: Which countries would be the happiest in the future? We used data from The World Happiness Reports and linear regression to find the answer. While we were working, we decided to also determine which countries would be the least happy as well which countries would make the largest changes both positively and negatively. Answering these questions have global implications since governments, organizations, and civil societies increasingly use happiness indicators to inform their policy-making decisions. Unfortunately, The World Happiness Reports only span from 2015-2020 so we felt it would be irresponsible to estimate further forward than we could go back, therefore our date was 5 years in the future, 2025.

We used a train-test split and linear regression to find an accuracy of .74.
